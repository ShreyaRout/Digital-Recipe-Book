Breakfast

Breakfast sets the tone for the day, and our collection of recipes is designed to offer the perfect balance between taste and nutrition. Whether you're in a rush or have time to enjoy a relaxing morning, you'll find a variety of wholesome, easy-to-make options that will leave you energized and ready to take on the day. Here, you'll find a variety of breakfast recipes, perfect for starting your day with a healthy meal that both satisfies your cravings and supports your well-being.

Beetroot Curd Salad

Beetroot Curd Salad is a refreshing, healthy dish combining the earthy sweetness of beetroot with the cool creaminess of curd. This vibrant salad is packed with nutrients, offering a delightful balance of flavors and textures, making it a perfect light meal or side dish.

Ragi Dosa

Ragi dosa is a nutritious South Indian pancake made from ragi (finger millet) flour. It’s a healthier alternative to traditional dosa, with a slightly earthy flavor and a crispy texture. Ragi is rich in calcium, iron, and dietary fiber, making this dosa a great option for a wholesome breakfast or snack. The batter typically includes ragi flour, rice flour, and spices, and it's cooked on a hot griddle until golden brown and crispy.

Poha

Poha is a popular Indian breakfast dish made from flattened rice, also known as beaten rice. It is light and easy to prepare, typically cooked with onions, green chilies, mustard seeds, turmeric, and garnished with fresh coriander and lemon juice. Poha is often enjoyed with a side of peanuts or sev and is known for its delightful combination of flavors and textures. It’s a comforting and nutritious option that’s perfect for a quick meal.

Uttapam

Uttapam is a savory South Indian pancake made from a fermented batter of rice and urad dal (black gram). Unlike regular dosas, uttapams are thicker and topped with various ingredients like onions, tomatoes, green chilies, and cilantro before being cooked on a griddle. The result is a soft, fluffy, and flavorful dish with a slightly crispy bottom. Uttapams are commonly served with coconut chutney and sambar, making them a hearty and satisfying meal.
